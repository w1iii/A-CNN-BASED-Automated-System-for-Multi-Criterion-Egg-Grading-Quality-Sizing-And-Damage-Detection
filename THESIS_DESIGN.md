# Design

The automated egg grading system aims to support sustainable poultry production by utilizing computer vision and artificial intelligence to evaluate egg quality across multiple physical criteria. As illustrated in Figure 2, the system will follow a multi-stage pipeline: real-time camera feed will capture images of eggs in a controlled environment, which will then be processed through a YOLOv8 Small (YOLOv8s) Convolutional Neural Network for object detection. The YOLOv8 model will be fine-tuned on a custom egg dataset with a 70-20-10 train-validation-test split over 20 epochs, using SGD optimizer (learning rate=0.01, batch size=16, momentum=0.937). Detected objects will be filtered using confidence thresholds (YOLO: 0.65-0.75, CNN: 0.60-0.70) and tracked using centroid-based object tracking to prevent duplicate counting, with unique IDs assigned to each egg.

Figure 2: Integrated Flow Design for Multi-Criteria Egg Grading to Support Sustainable Poultry Production

Detected and tracked eggs will be passed into a multi-criteria classification layer consisting of two components. First, a custom CNN architecture (`EggGradingCNN`) with two convolutional layers (3→32→64 channels) will perform damage classification (Damaged/Not Damaged) on 224×224 resized crops, trained over 20 epochs with Adam optimizer (learning rate=0.001). Second, a size categorization module will calculate egg dimensions using camera calibration (pixel-to-millimeter conversion), categorizing eggs into Small (<50mm), Medium (50-60mm), and Large (>60mm), with weight estimated using the formula W = 0.05 × D³ (clamped to 30-80g range). Results exceeding confidence thresholds will be compiled and assigned grades: Grade A (Not Damaged + Large), Grade B (Not Damaged + Medium), Grade C (Not Damaged + Small), or Reject (any Damaged egg).

A Python-based pipeline will aggregate the multi-criteria results and export the final grade and defect report in JSON format (e.g., `{"grade": "A", "size": "Large", "weight_g": 68.5, "damage_status": "Not Damaged"}`), which will be displayed on the real-time video feed with color-coded bounding boxes (green for Not Damaged, red for Damaged) and logged into `egg_statistics.csv` for inventory management. The system will process at approximately 28-30 FPS, reducing false positives by ~70% through confidence filtering. By automating egg grading, the system will support sustainable poultry production through reduced food waste, optimized resource allocation, and improved labor efficiency.
